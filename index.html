About Me

I'm Grant Kee, and I love learning new things.

I specialize in backend web development.

My Experience

My bread and butter. I enjoy challenging myself with advanced React techniques, and it doesn't get much easier than using Express.js and Node to whip up a server. I recently completed Andrew Ng's machine learning course through Coursera, and it changed my life. I've decided to keep learning by exploring a sub-categroy of machine learning called Deeplearning. I'm focused on vision applications using TensorFlow using a Jetson Nano.

My primary role as a backend developer at Fractal Networks is designing and building microservices. After a successful, albeit quite, launch of Fractal Link I shifted focus to design and build the company's main product (which uses Fractal Link). The proof-of-concept (PoC) is complete, and I'm working to expand features for an alpha release in the fall of 2022.

Working on a small team of 6 requires that I wear a lot of hats. I currently serve as a backend engineer, architect, devops engineer, member of the leadership council creating the company's roadmap and business strategy, and I'm the acting scrum master. I thrive in high-demanding environments and love fast-paced work environments. My team has greatly benefited from embracing an agile framework, like scrum.

I primarily work with Rust and Django, depending on the service. For internal-only and heavy-duty services, I prefer to code in Rust. For services that I work on with younger devs, I use Django. All of the services used are published privately using GitLab's Container Registry.

The focus of my time with Fractal Networks has been how to bring about a more decentralized internet. The solution we came up with was helping users overcome the technical experience required for self-hosting. Our idea is if self-hosting becomes more reliable and easier to accomplish, more users will adopt this approach and the internet will become more decentralized. The path forward has not been easy, but we've taken on the challenges head-first by ensuring our users security, failover safety, and making it possible to self-host collaboratively. Your data should be yours, not the cloud provider's.

After nearly a decade of success, Mathalicious decided to rebrand to Citizen Math. They were working with a small firm to develop the new site, but after 6 months of constant back and forth, the project had barely progressed. It was clear that they needed a fresh start with someone who would take the rebranding seriously.

I worked closely with two non-technical stakeholders and a design team to bring their vision for Citizen Math to life. My resposibilities were to handle every technical aspect of the new site, including the database structure, processing payments through Stripe, the registration process, and more. As a team player, I was careful not to unilaterally alter the project's scope or say something couldn't be done. Instead, I did my best to start a conversation that helped the business decide what the right balance of scope, cost, and time should be moving forward. This lead to a much more collaborative process that ensured all parties were confident in the decision moving forward.
